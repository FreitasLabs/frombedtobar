Strawberry Basil Smash

Muddled fresh basil, sun-ripened strawberries, vodka, and lemon. Two ingredients that peak at the same time and belong together in the glass. Simple to make, impossible to put down.

🌱 Basil 🍓 Strawberry Vodka or Gin Spring–Summer 5 min

The Recipe

Strawberry Basil Smash

Both versions use the same muddled base. The cocktail adds vodka; the mocktail keeps the full fruit-and-herb character with sparkling water and a touch of grape juice for body. Double-strain both — a fine mesh strainer is non-negotiable with a smash.

  • 2 oz vodka (or gin for more depth)
  • 4–5 fresh strawberries, hulled and quartered
  • 🌱 4 fresh basil leaves
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 2 oz soda water
  • Garnish: basil leaf and a strawberry slice
  • 4–5 fresh strawberries, hulled and quartered
  • 🌱 4 fresh basil leaves
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 1 oz white grape juice
  • 3 oz sparkling water
  • Garnish: basil leaf and a strawberry slice
Add basil leaves to the shaker first, then pile the quartered strawberries on top. Muddle firmly until the strawberries are fully broken down and the basil is bruised — about 5 firm presses. Add vodka (or skip for mocktail), grape juice (mocktail only), lemon juice, and simple syrup. Fill with ice and shake hard for 12–15 seconds. Double-strain through a hawthorne strainer and fine mesh strainer into a rocks glass over fresh ice. Top with soda water. Garnish with a slapped basil leaf and a halved strawberry on the rim.

In the Garden

Growing Basil & Strawberries

Basil and strawberries don't just taste good together — they're ready at the same time. Both hit their peak in late spring and early summer, which makes this the most naturally timed drink in the bar garden. See the Basil Growing Guide for everything from seedling to syrup.

🌱
Basil in Season
Plant basil after the last frost when nights stay consistently above 50°F. It grows fast in warm weather — from seedling to first harvest in 3 to 4 weeks. Pinch flower buds the moment they appear to keep the leaves coming. Cold or stress makes basil bitter; warm soil and consistent watering make it sweet.
🍓
Strawberries for the Bar
June-bearing strawberries are the sweetest for cocktail use — they produce one concentrated crop of intensely flavored berries in late spring. Everbearing types are more convenient but the individual berries are less sweet. For this drink, the deeper the red, the better. Fully ripe strawberries muddle down to almost nothing — that's what you want.
✂️
Harvesting Basil Right
Always cut from the top of the plant, just above a pair of leaves. This forces the plant to branch and produce more growth below. For muddling, pick the largest, most fragrant leaves — usually the middle leaves of each stem, not the young tips. Morning harvesting gives you the most aromatic leaves before the sun cooks off the volatile oils.

Bartender Notes

Tips for getting it right

Muddling
Basil goes in first, strawberries on top
This matters more than it sounds. By layering basil underneath and pressing the fruit down into it, the strawberry juice helps extract the basil oils without over-shredding the leaves. Press firmly 4 to 5 times — the strawberries should be completely broken down before you add ice and spirit.
Straining
Double-strain every time — no exceptions
A hawthorne strainer catches the big pieces. A fine mesh strainer catches everything else — basil fragments, seeds, and fine strawberry pulp. Skipping the second strainer gives you a drink with visible debris that tastes slightly bitter from the basil stems. Takes two extra seconds. Always worth it.
Sweetness
Taste your strawberries before measuring
In-season strawberries at peak ripeness are much sweeter than off-season ones. If your berries are very sweet, reduce the simple syrup to ¼ oz. If they're tart, keep the full ½ oz and possibly add a little more. The drink should finish clean and tart, not sweet and heavy.
Garnish
Slap the basil leaf before you place it
Hold the basil leaf flat between both palms and clap once firmly. You'll hear the oils release. This turns an inert leaf into a fragrant garnish that delivers aroma with every sip. Drop it flat on the surface of the drink — don't skewer it or stand it up on the rim.

Serve It With

Food & Occasion Pairings

🧀
Food
Burrata with strawberries and balsamic. Prosciutto and cantaloupe. Caprese salad. Strawberry shortcake. Anything light and summery — this drink wants food with brightness, not weight.
☀️
Occasion
Garden parties, weekend brunches, porch evenings in late May and June. This is the drink for the first warm Saturday of the year when the strawberries finally arrive at the farmers market and the basil is big enough to pick.
🕐
Best Time
Afternoon through early evening. The brightness of the strawberry and lemon is best with some sun still in the sky. This drink was made for a spring afternoon on a porch or in the garden.
