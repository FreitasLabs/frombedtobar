Raw Egg Advisory

Some recipes on this website call for raw egg white as an ingredient, typically to create foam in cocktails such as whiskey sours and gin fizzes.

Warning: Consuming raw or undercooked eggs may increase the risk of foodborne illness caused by Salmonella or other bacteria. This risk is elevated for pregnant women, young children, the elderly, and individuals with compromised immune systems.

To reduce this risk, we recommend using pasteurized eggs, which are available at most grocery stores. All recipes on this site that include raw egg white also offer aquafaba (the liquid from a can of chickpeas) as a safe, effective, egg-free alternative that produces equivalent foam.

From Bed to Bar is not responsible for any illness or adverse effects resulting from the use of raw eggs in our recipes.

Garden & Foraging Safety

From Bed to Bar includes guides for growing, harvesting, and using garden plants, herbs, edible flowers, and foraged ingredients such as elderflower in cocktails and syrups.

  • Always positively identify any plant before consuming it. Some plants that resemble edible varieties are toxic.
  • Elderflower (Sambucus nigra) flowers are safe when properly prepared, but raw elderberries and other parts of the plant are mildly toxic and must be cooked before consumption.
  • Some edible flowers and herbs may cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
  • Do not forage from areas that may have been treated with pesticides, herbicides, or other chemicals.
  • Consult a qualified botanist or foraging expert if you are uncertain about the identity of any plant.

From Bed to Bar is not responsible for any illness, injury, or adverse effects resulting from the use of plants, herbs, or foraged ingredients based on the guidance provided on this website.
