Sparkling Mint
Limeade

Fresh garden mint syrup, real lime juice, and sparkling water. Three ingredients, five minutes, and one of the best things to come out of the garden all summer — with or without a spirit alongside it.

🌿 Alcohol-Free ⏱ 5 minutes 🌱 From the garden All Ages

The recipe

Sparkling Mint Limeade

This is a two-step drink — make the mint simple syrup once, then build this in under five minutes whenever you want it. The syrup keeps for three weeks in the fridge, which means you can have this drink any afternoon without touching the garden again. For growing the mint, see the full Mint Guide.

Step 01

Fill your glass with ice

Pack a tall glass with ice — enough that the liquid will sit just below the rim when you're done. A highball or a pint glass both work. Chilling the glass first for a minute in the freezer is optional but extends how long the drink stays cold.

Step 02

Add mint syrup and lime juice

Pour ¾ oz of homegrown mint simple syrup over the ice, followed by ¾ oz of fresh-squeezed lime juice. Stir briefly — 5 to 10 seconds — to combine and start chilling the mixture.

Taste the syrup-lime mixture before adding the soda. If you want more mint, add another ¼ oz of syrup. If it's too sweet, add a squeeze more lime. Fix the balance before the bubbles go in.

Step 03

Top with sparkling water

Pour 4 oz of sparkling water slowly down the inside of the glass. This preserves the carbonation — pouring straight down over the ice knocks out most of the bubbles before you take a sip. One gentle stir from the bottom to integrate everything without deflating the drink.

Step 04

Garnish with fresh mint

Take a fresh sprig of spearmint from the garden. Slap it firmly against your palm to bruise the leaves and release the aromatic oils, then tuck it into the top of the drink. Add a lime wheel on the rim if you have one to spare.

The garnish mint isn't decoration — you'll smell it with every sip. This is where garden-fresh mint makes the most obvious difference. Cut the sprig right before serving.

On the sparkling water

The quality of the sparkling water matters more than you'd think in a drink this simple. A still mineral water with added carbonation tastes flat next to a naturally sparkling one like Topo Chico, Gerolsteiner, or San Pellegrino. The mineral content adds a slight salinity that sharpens the mint and lifts the lime. If you only have standard soda water, it works — but try the upgrade once and you'll notice.

Ingredients

  • Mint simple syrup ¾ oz
  • Fresh lime juice ¾ oz
  • Sparkling water 4 oz
  • Fresh mint sprig 1 — garnish
  • Lime wheel 1 — garnish
Glass & Ice Highball or pint glass packed with ice. Serve immediately after adding the sparkling water.
Cocktail version Add 2 oz of white rum and reduce sparkling water to 2 oz. See the Garden Mojito for the full recipe.

Make it your own

Variations

This drink changes completely when you change the syrup or add one ingredient. All of these use the same method — just swap or add one thing.

Mint & Cucumber Limeade
Use cucumber mint syrup (add 6 thin cucumber slices to your mint steep). Add 2 cucumber rounds to the glass with the ice. The coolest thing you'll drink all summer.
Mint Limeade with Coconut
Replace 2 oz of the sparkling water with chilled coconut water. Slightly sweet, subtly tropical, and excellent in hot weather. Works beautifully with a splash of fresh ginger too.
Spicy Mint Limeade
Use spicy mint syrup — made by adding jalapeño to the steep. The heat appears slowly after the mint and citrus, and it lingers pleasantly. Better than it sounds on paper.
Mint & Elderflower
Add ¼ oz of elderflower cordial (St. Germain or non-alcoholic cordial) to the base. Floral and fragrant — particularly nice in late spring or with a few fresh flower garnishes.
Frozen Mint Limeade
Blend all ingredients with 1 cup of ice until smooth. Serve immediately in a wide glass. The texture is somewhere between a slushy and a granita — excellent for outdoor entertaining.
Honey Mint Limeade
Use honey mint syrup (replace sugar with raw honey in the syrup recipe). Richer and more complex — particularly good with a pinch of sea salt and a few fresh thyme leaves in the glass.

For a crowd

Scaling Up for a Batch

This drink is easy to make by the pitcher. Mix the syrup and lime juice ahead of time and refrigerate — add the sparkling water only when serving so you don't lose the carbonation. Garnish each glass individually with garden mint.

Serves 4
Small Pitcher
  • 3 oz mint syrup
  • 3 oz fresh lime juice
  • 16 oz sparkling water
  • Large handful of ice
Serves 8
Large Pitcher
  • 6 oz mint syrup
  • 6 oz fresh lime juice
  • 32 oz sparkling water
  • Plenty of ice
Serves 20+
Party Dispenser
  • 15 oz mint syrup
  • 15 oz fresh lime juice
  • 4 × 1L sparkling water
  • Add soda per glass
