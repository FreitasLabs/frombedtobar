Cucumber Mint Gin & Tonic

The garden gin and tonic — fresh cucumber ribbons, muddled mint from the bed, dry gin, and a quality tonic poured slowly over the top. No ice bucket required. Just a garden that's doing its job.

The Recipe

Cucumber Mint Gin & Tonic

This is a built drink — no shaker, no strain. Everything goes into a tall glass in order. The key is cold ice, quality tonic, and not over-muddling the mint. The mocktail version uses a non-alcoholic spirit or a more flavorful tonic with elderflower — both are genuinely worth making.

  • 2 oz London Dry gin
  • 🥒 3–4 thin cucumber slices
  • 🌿 4 fresh mint leaves
  • 4 oz quality tonic water
  • ½ oz fresh lime juice
  • Ice — plenty of it
  • Garnish: cucumber ribbon + mint sprig
  • 🥒 3–4 thin cucumber slices
  • 🌿 4 fresh mint leaves
  • ½ oz elderflower cordial
  • ½ oz fresh lime juice
  • 5 oz quality tonic water
  • Ice — plenty of it
  • Garnish: cucumber ribbon + mint sprig
Add cucumber slices and mint to the bottom of a tall highball glass. Press once gently with a muddler — enough to bruise the mint and release the cucumber's juice, not to shred everything into pulp. Fill the glass completely with ice, packing it high. Pour in the gin (or skip for mocktail). Add lime juice and elderflower cordial if using. Pour tonic slowly down the inside wall of the glass — this preserves the bubbles. Stir once gently from the bottom with a bar spoon. Garnish with a long cucumber ribbon draped over the rim and a full mint sprig pressed against the inside of the glass so it frames the drink.

In the Garden

Growing Mint & Cucumbers

Mint and cucumbers both peak in midsummer and together they make the most refreshing drink in the bar garden. The one rule with mint: contain it. See the Mint Growing Guide for everything from varieties to harvest and the Cocktail Herb Hub for the full picture.

🌿
Mint for the Bar
Spearmint and Kentish mint are the best cocktail varieties — clean and bright, not medicinal. Grow in a container or sink a pot into the ground to prevent it spreading. Harvest in the morning before the sun cooks off the volatile oils. Pinch flowers as they form to keep leaves coming through the season.
🥒
Cucumbers in Season
English and Persian cucumbers are the best for cocktails — thin skin, mild flavor, no bitterness. Plant in late spring, trellis them vertically if space is tight, and harvest when they're firm and deeply green. Cucumbers left too long on the vine turn yellow and bitter. Pick early, pick often.
🧊
Using Both at Peak
July is when both mint and cucumbers are at their best simultaneously. This is the drink that captures that intersection — cool, green, and deeply refreshing in a way that's impossible to replicate with anything from a bottle. Make it when both are fresh from the garden and the ice is cold.

Bartender Notes

Tips for getting it right

Muddling
One press — then stop
This drink lives and dies by the muddling. One firm press of the cucumber and mint together releases enough flavor without shredding the mint into bitter fragments. Two presses is the absolute maximum. Over-muddled mint turns the drink dark and medicinal. The goal is a bruise, not a paste.
Ice
Fill the glass to the top
A G&T needs to stay cold all the way to the last sip. Pack the glass completely with ice before adding any liquid. Large ice cubes melt more slowly than small ones — if you have them, use them. A well-iced G&T stays at temperature for 20 minutes; an under-iced one is warm and flat by the time you get halfway through it.
Tonic
Pour down the side, never from the top
Pouring tonic straight into the glass knocks the bubbles out and gives you a flat drink within minutes. Tilt the glass slightly and pour the tonic slowly down the inside wall — the bubbles survive and the drink stays lively to the last sip. This applies to any carbonated drink built in a glass.
Gin Selection
Match the botanical weight to the glass
This is a delicate, green, garden-forward drink. A heavy, juniper-dominant gin can overwhelm the cucumber and mint. Look for a gin with a lighter, more aromatic botanical profile — or use exactly 2 oz of a classic London Dry and let the garden ingredients lead. Hendrick's was designed for this exact purpose.

Serve It With

Food & Occasion Pairings

🥗
Food
Smoked salmon and cucumber on blinis. Tzatziki and pita. Light summer salads. Sushi. Anything cold, fresh, and delicate — this drink is too clean and bright to pair with anything heavy or rich.
🌞
Occasion
The afternoon garden drink. The thing you make when someone shows up unexpectedly on a hot day. Backyard gatherings, poolside, the first drink of a long summer evening. Easy to make for a crowd — set out the ingredients and let people build their own.
🌅
Best Time
Any time the temperature is above 80°F. Specifically: 4 PM on a July afternoon when the garden is lush and the ice is cold and there is absolutely nothing wrong with the world.
