Homemade Plum Jam

From the tree to the jar — a full canning guide built around the plums in your own backyard.

⏱ About 1 hour 🫙 5–6 pints 🌿 From the garden 🥃 Makes cocktail syrup too
Jump to: Prepare Cook Add Sugar Jam Stage Test Finishing Canning Storage

There's something about a backyard plum harvest that demands you do something with it. The trees don't ask whether you're ready — they just ripen. This jam is the answer for most of the fruit.

It's a straightforward put-up: plums, sugar, lemon juice, and time. The cinnamon and vanilla are optional but recommended. The result keeps for a year on the shelf, gets better on toast after about two weeks, and — if you hold back a pound or two before the sugar goes in — becomes the base for Plum Simple Syrup and the Plum Bourbon Sour.

Recommended Version
  • 5.6 lb pitted plums
  • 3.75 lb granulated sugar
  • Juice of 2 lemons, about 1/4 cup
  • 1 cinnamon stick (during cooking)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (after cooking)

This combination gives you a rich, balanced plum flavor without pushing into candy-sweet. The cinnamon is subtle enough that you won't identify it — you'll just notice the jam tastes a little more like itself.

Step 1 — Prepare the Fruit

  1. Wash plums thoroughly under cold water.
  2. Pit and cut into quarters or rough chunks. No need to peel — the skins add body and color.
  3. Add to a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven.
Garden to glass note Before adding anything else, set aside 1 lb of cut fruit in a separate pot. That becomes your Plum Simple Syrup — same harvest, two products.

Step 2 — Cook the Fruit

  1. Add lemon juice and cinnamon stick if using.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. After 15–20 minutes the fruit should be fully soft and beginning to break down. Press gently with your spoon to help it along.

Step 3 — Add Sugar

  1. Gradually stir in sugar. Take your time — rushing this can cause scorching on the bottom.
  2. Stir until the sugar is fully dissolved before you increase the heat.
  3. Bring to a full rolling boil that can't be stirred down.

Step 4 — Cook to Jam Stage

  1. Boil vigorously, stirring frequently. The mixture will spit — keep the heat managed and stay close.
  2. Continue cooking 20–35 minutes. The range depends on how much natural moisture your plums had.
  3. Skim foam from the surface if desired. This is cosmetic — it doesn't affect the flavor or safety of the jam.

Step 5 — Test for Doneness

  1. Before you start cooking, put a small plate in the freezer.
  2. When you think the jam might be done, drop a small spoonful onto the cold plate.
  3. Wait 30 seconds, then push the edge with your finger.
  4. If the surface wrinkles and holds its shape, it's ready. If it runs, give it another 5 minutes and test again.
No thermometer needed The plate test is reliable. If you have a candy thermometer, you're looking for 220°F (104°C) at sea level. Adjust down about 2°F for every 1,000 feet of elevation.

Finishing the Jam

  1. Remove from heat and pull out the cinnamon stick.
  2. Stir in vanilla extract, almond extract, or bourbon if using. Adding these off-heat preserves more of their flavor.
  3. Let stand 5 minutes, then stir gently before filling jars. This helps distribute the fruit evenly.

Water-Bath Canning

  1. Ladle hot jam into hot sterilized jars, leaving 1/4-inch headspace.
  2. Wipe jar rims clean with a damp cloth. Any residue on the rim can prevent a proper seal.
  3. Apply lids and bands fingertip tight — firm but not cranked down.
  4. Process half-pint or pint jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  5. Remove jars and let cool undisturbed for 12–24 hours. You'll hear the lids pop as they seal.
Altitude adjustment Processing times assume sea level. If you're above 1,000 feet, adjust according to current USDA guidelines — add 1 minute for every 1,000 feet up to 6,000 feet, then 2 minutes per 1,000 feet above that.

Ingredients Scaled

Scale this recipe
lb pitted plums

Scales plums, sugar, and lemon juice proportionally. Optional additions and canning method stay the same.

Base Recipe

  • 5.6 lb (2.54 kg) pitted plums
  • 3.75 lb (1.7 kg) granulated sugar
  • Juice of 2 lemons, about 1/4 cup

Optional Additions

  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp almond extract
  • 1–2 Tbsp bourbon
  • 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger
Canning safety note Scaling changes ingredient amounts only. Processing time, headspace, and jar size must follow current USDA guidelines regardless of batch size. Do not reduce headspace or change jar size when scaling up or down.
Yield 5.6 lb pitted plums yields 5–6 pints or 8–10 half-pint jars.

Equipment

  • Large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven
  • Long-handled spoon
  • Potato masher (optional)
  • Candy thermometer (optional)
  • Sterilized canning jars and lids
  • Water-bath canner
  • Jar lifter and canning funnel
