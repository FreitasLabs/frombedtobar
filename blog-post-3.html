5 Cocktails You Can Make with
What's Growing Right Now

May 28, 2026  ·  4 min read

Late May is one of the best weeks of the year for a garden bar. Strawberries are at peak. Mint is growing faster than you can use it. Basil is finally warm enough to be happy. Lavender is blooming. Elderflower, if you can find it, is in its brief but brilliant window.

Here are five drinks built entirely around what's harvestable right now — no out-of-season ingredients, no substitutions. Each one has a mocktail version so everyone at the table has something worth drinking.

1. Strawberry Basil Smash

The combination of strawberry and basil shouldn't work as well as it does. The basil adds an herbal, almost peppery note that keeps the strawberry from being cloying. This is the drink to make when your strawberry plants are at peak and your basil has leafed out.

Strawberry Basil Smash — Cocktail
  • 2 oz vodka
  • 4–5 fresh strawberries, hulled
  • 4 fresh basil leaves
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 2 oz soda water
Muddle strawberries and basil in a shaker. Add lemon juice, simple syrup, and vodka. Fill with ice and shake well. Strain into a rocks glass over ice, top with soda water. Garnish with a basil leaf and strawberry slice.

Mocktail: Skip the vodka. Add 1 oz white grape juice and increase soda water to 3 oz.

2. Lavender Lemon Spritz

If you have lavender blooming right now, make syrup this week. The flowers have a short window and the syrup keeps for three weeks in the fridge. This spritz is light, floral, and works as well before dinner as it does on a hot afternoon.

Lavender Lemon Spritz — Cocktail
  • 1 oz vodka
  • ¾ oz lavender syrup
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • 3 oz prosecco
  • Splash of soda water
Combine vodka, lavender syrup, and lemon juice in a shaker with ice. Shake and strain into a wine glass over ice. Top with prosecco and a splash of soda. Garnish with a lavender sprig and lemon wheel.

Mocktail: Skip vodka and prosecco. Use 4 oz sparkling water, ¾ oz lavender syrup, ¾ oz lemon, 1 tsp honey.

3. Cucumber Mint Gin and Tonic

Mint is in full swing and cucumbers will be ready in a few weeks — but if you have them now, this is the drink. Gin and cucumber have a natural affinity, and fresh mint bridges them beautifully. Don't overdo the muddling: you want to release, not shred.

Cucumber Mint G&T — Cocktail
  • 2 oz gin
  • 4 thin cucumber slices
  • 6 fresh mint leaves
  • ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  • 4 oz quality tonic water
Muddle cucumber and mint gently in a glass — don't overwork. Fill with ice, add gin and lime, top with tonic. One slow stir. Garnish with a cucumber ribbon and mint sprig.

Mocktail: Use non-alcoholic gin base or skip entirely. Add ½ oz simple syrup to compensate for sweetness.

4. Elderflower and Cucumber Gin Fizz

This one requires elderflower syrup — which you should be making right now if you have access to elderflowers. The combination of gin, elderflower, and cucumber is one of the great spring flavor combinations. The egg white (or aquafaba) gives it a silky foam top.

Elderflower Gin Fizz — Cocktail
  • 1½ oz gin
  • ¾ oz elderflower liqueur or syrup
  • 4 thin cucumber slices
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 1 egg white* or ½ oz aquafaba
  • 2 oz soda water
Muddle cucumber. Add remaining ingredients except soda. Dry shake 15 seconds, add ice, shake again. Strain into a chilled glass. Top slowly with soda — the foam will rise. Garnish with a cucumber slice.

Mocktail: Use elderflower tonic instead of liqueur. Skip gin. Use aquafaba for foam.

5. Blueberry Mint Mojito

Blueberries hit in June through August depending on your zone — but if you have them now, or if you froze last year's crop, this mojito is exceptional. Mint is the backbone. Fresh lemon instead of lime gives it a slightly softer edge that pairs better with blueberry.

Blueberry Mint Mojito — Cocktail
  • 2 oz white rum
  • ½ cup fresh blueberries
  • 8 fresh mint leaves
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 3 oz soda water
Muddle blueberries and mint in a shaker. Add lemon, simple syrup, and rum. Fill with ice and shake well. Strain into a tall glass over ice, top with soda. Garnish with mint and a few whole blueberries.

Mocktail: Skip rum. Use coconut water or sparkling water as the base — both work beautifully.

Raw Egg Advisory: Some recipes on this page contain raw egg white. Consuming raw or undercooked eggs may increase the risk of foodborne illness, particularly for pregnant women, young children, the elderly, and those with compromised immune systems. Use pasteurized eggs to reduce this risk, or substitute aquafaba (chickpea liquid) as an egg-free alternative.
