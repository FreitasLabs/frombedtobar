About From Bed to Bar

Garden-grown drinks,
made approachable.

From Bed to Bar is a garden-to-glass project built around one simple idea: the best drinks start outside, not at a bottle shop. If you grow it, you can pour it — and it doesn't have to be complicated.

The origin

Why I Started From Bed to Bar

I'm Stephanie. I'm a home gardener who also enjoys a well-made drink — and for a long time, those two things felt completely separate. I'd grow mint that I didn't know what to do with. I'd pick lavender and let it dry out on the counter. I had a lemon verbena plant that sat there looking beautiful and entirely unused.

At some point it clicked that everything I was growing was already in cocktail recipes — it just wasn't labeled that way. The mint in my raised bed was a mojito waiting to happen. The rosemary by the back fence was a sour looking for a bourbon. The elderflower I almost ripped out was practically a spritz on its own.

From Bed to Bar started as a way for me to document what I was figuring out: which herbs work well in drinks, how to turn a backyard harvest into something worth pouring, and how to do all of it without needing a professional bar setup or a bartending certificate. It's grown from there, but that's still the heart of it — practical, garden-first, honest about what actually works at home.

What's here

What You'll Find Here

From Bed to Bar is built for home gardeners, beginners at the bar, and anyone who wants to actually use what they grow. You don't need a lot of equipment. You don't need an impressive spirits collection. You need a garden — even a small one — and some curiosity.

  • 🌿
    Cocktail and mocktail recipes built around real garden ingredients — herbs, edible flowers, fruit, and homegrown syrups. Every recipe includes a non-alcoholic version, because garden-forward drinks don't need alcohol to be worth making.
  • 🪴
    Garden tips for growing plants that are genuinely useful at the bar — including which varieties are worth the effort, how to harvest without killing the plant, and how to preserve what you grow.
  • 📅
    A zone-by-zone planting guide so you can plan what to grow, when to sow, and what's likely to thrive in your climate.
  • 🍋

  • 📖
    Blog posts on seasonal ingredients, what's growing in the garden right now, and the practical side of going garden-to-glass at home.

The approach

How Recipes Are Built

Every recipe on this site starts with the garden, not the spirit. That changes the way drinks get developed — it's seasonal first, substitution-friendly, and realistic about what home gardeners actually have on hand.

01
Real ingredients, not idealized ones. Recipes are built around what you can actually grow — not obscure botanical extracts or ingredients you'd only find at a specialty importer. If a plant is in the recipe, it's one you can reasonably grow at home or find at a farmers' market.
02
Seasonal thinking baked in. What you can pour in May isn't the same as what you can pour in October. Recipes are written with seasonality in mind, and I try to be clear about when something is at its best and what you can substitute when it's not.
03
Approachable technique. Drinks here use methods a home cook can follow — muddling, shaking, simple syrups, infusions. If a technique requires specialised bar equipment, I'll say so and offer a workaround.
04
Mocktails are first-class. Every recipe includes a non-alcoholic version — not as an afterthought, but developed alongside the original. The garden flavours are the point; the spirit is optional.
05
Honest about limitations. I'm not a trained bartender or a professional recipe developer. These recipes reflect what works in my garden and kitchen. If something could go multiple ways or depends on your specific ingredients, I'll say that plainly rather than pretending there's one perfect version.

Important to know

A Note on Gardening, Foraging & Safety

Growing your own ingredients is one of the most rewarding parts of the garden-to-glass approach. But it comes with responsibility — especially when those ingredients end up in what you eat and drink.

Please keep these points in mind:

🌱 Garden & foraging safety

  • Identify plants correctly before you use them. Many edible plants have toxic lookalikes. If you're foraging rather than growing your own, be certain of what you're picking. When in doubt, don't use it.
  • Only use food-safe, edible plants. Not everything that looks like a herb is safe to consume. Stick to verified edible varieties, and cross-check before trying anything unfamiliar.
  • Avoid sprayed or chemically treated plants. Don't use plants that have been treated with pesticides, herbicides, or other chemicals not approved for food use. Grow your own where you can, and know the source when you don't.
  • Some plants cause reactions in some people. Allergies to herbs, flowers, and botanicals are real. If you or your guests have known sensitivities, check ingredients carefully.

If you're ever unsure about plant identification or food safety, the right call is to leave it out. No cocktail is worth a health risk.

On alcohol: every recipe on this site includes a mocktail version. If you're making the alcoholic version, please drink responsibly, know your limits, and never drive after drinking. This site is intended for adults of legal drinking age. See our full disclaimer for more.
